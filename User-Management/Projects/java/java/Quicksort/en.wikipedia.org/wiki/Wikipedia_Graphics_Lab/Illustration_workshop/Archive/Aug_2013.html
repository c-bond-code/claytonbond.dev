The background of the flag is green, symbolizing the growth, vitality and fertility of the land. The center emblem is a shield of white on which the colors red, blue and yellow are used. I have vectorized files and . Should I upload them to Commons with the same names and licenses? Or change something? Thanks, Hosmich (talk) 15:57, 5 August 2013 (UTC)[reply]

Generally, yes, I would upload the conversion of foo.png as foo.svg at Commons with the same license and a note in the file description of the source you redrew it from, like "redrawn from foo.png by Hosmich". Then tag foo.png with {{vva|foo.svg}} to indicate a vector version is available.
That, of course, assumes the licences are correct in the first place, and in this case I doubt they actually are correct since the uploaders on both have claimed to be the copyright holders - which seems impossible. They will certainly be free files as far as I can see, but there are some details missing at least on the "Symbol" one, and they probably both need fixing.
